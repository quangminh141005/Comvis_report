\section{Conclusion}

This project presented a receipt OCR pipeline combining image preprocessing with the
PP-OCRv4 framework, evaluated on the ICDAR 2019 SROIE dataset. The preprocessing module
effectively addressed common receipt degradations such as noise, poor contrast, and faded
ink, while the detection model achieved a strong precision of 93.83\% and F1-score of
91.62\%. At the recognition stage, the system reached a character-level accuracy of
87.96\%, with most remaining errors stemming from visually similar characters such as
\texttt{O/0} and \texttt{S/5}.

The gap between character and word-level accuracy reflects the inherent difficulty of
full-line transcription on real-world receipts, and points toward future improvements
such as attention-based decoders and post-processing correction layers. Overall, the
results demonstrate that combining classical preprocessing techniques with modern deep
learning produces a practical and effective system for automated receipt understanding.
